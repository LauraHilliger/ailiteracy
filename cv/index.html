Dr. Doug Belshaw

CV

Qualifications

Ed.D. (Doctor of Education) Durham University (2006-11)

PGCE (Secondary History) Durham University (2003-04)

MA (Modern History) Durham University (2002-03)

BA Hons. (Philosophy) Universityof Sheffield (1999-2002)

Co-founder, We Are Open Co-op (2016-Present)

Helping organisations work more openly and efficiently. After starting to sub-contract consultancy work, decided to set up a tech co-operative with friends and former Mozilla colleagues. Clients include Creative Commons, Greenpeace, and Red Hat.

Consultant, Dynamic Skillset (2015-Present)

Set up my own consultancy business and work with a range of organisations around Open Badges, digital/new literacies, and organisational change. 18-month contract with City & Guilds to create a ‘skunkworks’ within the organisation to explore digital workflows and a new badging platform.

Product Manager, Moodle (2018-2020)

Conceived the vision for, and built a team to deliver, the world’s first decentralised digital commons for educators. Part of the Moodle management team.

Web Literacy Lead, Mozilla Foundation (2013-2015)

Led Mozilla’s web literacy work which underpinned their Webmaker programme. Advised all parts of the organisation on initiatives relating to web literacy and defined a ‘standard’ for web literacy to build upon which involved more than 100 stakeholders worldwide.

Badges & Skills Lead, Mozilla Foundation (2012-2013)

Led Mozilla’s work in Europe around promoting a new, web-native standard for issuing and displaying credentials known as the ‘Open Badges Infrastructure’ or OBI. Spoke at 50+ conferences and events, driving adoption of the standard, which led to millions of dollars, thousands of students, and big brands such as IBM getting behind Open Badges.

Researcher/Analyst, Jisc infoNet (2010-2012)

Based at Northumbria University, worked on national programmes to create resources for use by senior managers in FE and HE institutions. Supported Jisc’s successful ‘Developing Digital Literacies’ programme and managed the Open Educational Resources (OERs) infoKit. Presented regularly at conferences.

Pre-2010

Teacher and senior leader in schools in Nottinghamshire, South Yorkshire, and Northumberland.
